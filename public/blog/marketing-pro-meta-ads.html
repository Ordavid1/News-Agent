Marketing Pro+: Meta Ads Management, Auto-Boost Rules & ROI Tracking

Organic reach on Facebook and Instagram continues to decline year over year, and paid amplification is no longer optional for brands that want to stay visible. Marketing Pro+ brings Meta Ads management directly into Configure News so you can create campaigns, boost high-performing posts, and monitor return on ad spend without ever leaving the platform you already use to publish content. In this deep-dive, we will walk through every feature, explain how auto-boost rules save you hours of manual optimization, and show you how to read the analytics dashboard to make smarter budget decisions.

1. What is Marketing Pro+?

Marketing Pro+ is an optional add-on for Configure News that unlocks a full-featured Meta Ads management layer on top of your existing social media automation workflow. Priced at $19 per month per connected ad account, it is available to users on any Configure News subscription tier. Your actual advertising spend is billed separately by Meta; the Marketing Pro+ fee covers the tooling, automation engine, and analytics infrastructure within Configure News.

The core idea is straightforward: you already use Configure News to generate and publish content to Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, and other platforms. Marketing Pro+ extends that workflow into paid distribution. Instead of switching to Meta Ads Manager, learning a separate interface, and manually promoting posts, you handle everything in one place. The add-on connects to the Meta Marketing API through your Facebook Business account and gives you a streamlined campaign builder, a one-click boost button on every published post, an automated boost engine driven by engagement rules, reusable audience templates with reach estimation, and a unified marketing analytics dashboard that ties ad spend back to performance.

Because Marketing Pro+ sits inside the same platform where your organic content lives, it can do things a standalone ads tool cannot. It knows which posts are performing well organically before you spend a cent, it can automatically amplify those posts the moment they cross an engagement threshold, and it can attribute results back to the original content strategy you defined in Configure News. This tight feedback loop between organic publishing and paid amplification is what makes the add-on genuinely useful rather than just another ads interface.

"The best ad budget is the one you spend on content that has already proven it resonates. Marketing Pro+ automates exactly that decision."

2. Creating Meta Ads Campaigns

Marketing Pro+ includes a campaign builder designed for speed. Rather than replicating the dozens of options inside Meta Ads Manager, it focuses on the settings that actually move the needle for social media marketers: objective, audience, budget, schedule, and creative.

Choosing a Campaign Objective

When you create a new campaign from the Configure News dashboard, the first step is selecting an objective. Marketing Pro+ supports the three objectives most relevant to content-driven advertising:

  • Awareness — Maximize impressions and reach for brand-building campaigns. Ideal when you want as many people as possible to see a piece of thought leadership or a product announcement.
  • Traffic — Drive clicks to a URL. Configure News automatically pulls the link from your published post, or you can override it with a custom destination such as a landing page or lead magnet.
  • Engagement — Optimize for reactions, comments, and shares. This objective is particularly effective when combined with auto-boost rules, because the system already knows the post is generating organic engagement and can compound that momentum with paid reach.

Setting Budget and Schedule

You can set either a daily budget or a lifetime budget for each campaign. Daily budgets are useful for ongoing evergreen campaigns, while lifetime budgets are better for time-bound promotions. The schedule picker lets you define start and end dates and, optionally, dayparting rules so your ads only run during peak hours for your target audience. Marketing Pro+ displays an estimated reach range based on your budget and audience size, so you can calibrate spend before launching.

Selecting Creative

Because you are working inside Configure News, your creative options are tightly integrated with your content library. You can select any previously published Facebook or Instagram post as the ad creative, upload a new image or video directly in the campaign builder, or let Configure News generate ad copy using the same AI engine that powers your organic posts. For Instagram campaigns, the builder enforces media requirements automatically: it will not let you launch an Instagram ad without an image or video attachment, and it validates aspect ratios and file sizes before submission.

Once you finalize the creative, audience, budget, and schedule, clicking "Launch Campaign" sends the campaign to the Meta Marketing API. Configure News monitors the submission status and notifies you if Meta's ad review flags any issues, so you can resolve them without checking a separate inbox.

3. Post Boosting

Not every paid promotion needs a full campaign. Sometimes you just want to put a few dollars behind a post that is performing well. Marketing Pro+ adds a "Boost" button to every published Facebook and Instagram post in your Configure News dashboard, making paid amplification a one-click action.

When you click Boost, a compact dialog opens with three settings: budget (default $5, adjustable in $1 increments up to your account limit), duration (1 to 14 days), and audience (choose from your saved audience templates or use Meta's default broad targeting). Confirm the boost, and Configure News handles the rest through the API. The boosted post appears in your marketing analytics alongside full campaigns, so you have a single view of all paid activity.

Boosting is intentionally simple by design. It is meant for quick, low-friction promotions when you spot an opportunity in real time. For systematic, rule-based promotion, the auto-boost engine described in the next section is the better tool.

A few practical tips for manual boosting:

  • Boost within the first 2 hours — Posts that receive early paid reach tend to perform better in organic algorithms as well, creating a compounding effect.
  • Use saved audience templates — Avoid relying on Meta's broad default. A well-defined audience template gives your budget more precision and typically lowers your cost per result.
  • Keep durations short — Social content has a short shelf life. A 2-to-3-day boost window captures most of the value without wasting budget on a stale post.
  • Review performance after 24 hours — If cost-per-click is above your target, pause the boost rather than letting it run to completion.

4. Auto-Boost Rules

Auto-boost rules are the flagship automation feature of Marketing Pro+. They let you define conditions under which Configure News will automatically promote an organic post without any manual intervention. The concept is simple: your organic content acts as a testing ground, and when a post proves it resonates with your audience, the system puts paid budget behind it to maximize its reach.

How Rules Work

Each rule consists of four components:

  1. Trigger metric — The engagement signal that activates the rule. Supported metrics include likes/reactions, comments, shares, saves, and link clicks. You can combine metrics with "AND" logic (e.g., 50+ likes AND 10+ comments).
  2. Threshold value — The numeric threshold that the metric must reach. For example, "50 likes" or "20 comments."
  3. Observation window — The time period after publishing during which Configure News monitors the post. Common windows are 1 hour, 2 hours, 4 hours, and 8 hours. Shorter windows catch viral spikes early; longer windows are better for audiences that engage throughout the day.
  4. Boost configuration — The budget, duration, and audience template to use when the rule fires. You can also set a daily cap so a single rule does not consume your entire monthly ad budget in one day.

Example Rule

Suppose you publish five Facebook posts per day and want to automatically amplify the top performers. You could create a rule that says: "If a post receives 50 or more likes within 4 hours of publishing, boost it for 3 days with a $10/day budget using my 'Tech Professionals 25-45' audience template, with a daily rule cap of $30." This means at most three posts per day will be auto-boosted, and your maximum daily spend from this rule is $30. Posts that do not hit the threshold are left as organic-only, and your budget is spent exclusively on content that has already demonstrated audience interest.

Best Practices for Auto-Boost Rules

  • Start with conservative thresholds — Set your initial trigger slightly above your average organic engagement so only genuinely above-average posts get boosted. You can lower the threshold later once you understand your typical performance range.
  • Use daily caps religiously — Without a cap, a day of unusually high engagement could trigger boosts on every post and drain your monthly budget.
  • Create platform-specific rules — Facebook and Instagram have different engagement patterns. A post that earns 50 likes on Facebook in 4 hours might be average, while the same number on Instagram could be exceptional. Define separate rules for each platform.
  • Pair with audience templates — Auto-boosted posts perform best when they reach a well-defined audience rather than a broad default. Always attach a saved audience template to your rule.
  • Review weekly — Check which posts triggered auto-boosts and analyze their paid performance. Use these insights to refine your thresholds and creative strategy.

5. Audience Templates

Effective advertising requires precise targeting, but configuring an audience from scratch every time you boost a post or launch a campaign is tedious and error-prone. Marketing Pro+ solves this with audience templates: reusable audience definitions that you build once and apply to any campaign or boost with a single click.

Building an Audience Template

The template builder exposes the targeting dimensions available through the Meta Marketing API in a simplified interface:

  • Location — Target by country, region, city, or radius around a postal code. You can include or exclude specific locations.
  • Age range — Set minimum and maximum age (18 to 65+).
  • Gender — Target all, male, or female audiences.
  • Interests — Browse or search Meta's interest taxonomy. Interests are organized by category (Business, Technology, Entertainment, Fitness, etc.) and you can add as many as needed.
  • Behaviors — Target users based on purchase behavior, device usage, travel habits, and more.
  • Custom audiences — If you have uploaded customer lists or installed the Meta Pixel, you can include or exclude custom audiences in your template.
  • Lookalike audiences — Build a template around a lookalike audience derived from your best customers or most engaged followers.

Reach Estimation

As you adjust targeting parameters, Marketing Pro+ displays a real-time estimated reach range powered by Meta's Reach Estimate API. This tells you approximately how many people your ad could reach per day at a given budget. If the estimated reach is too narrow (under 1,000), the interface warns you that performance may be limited and suggests broadening your targeting. If it is too broad (over 10 million), it suggests adding more specific interest or demographic filters to improve relevance.

Once saved, a template appears in the audience picker throughout Marketing Pro+: in the campaign builder, in the boost dialog, and in auto-boost rule configuration. You can create as many templates as you need. Common patterns include a broad awareness template, a narrow retargeting template, and several interest-based templates aligned with your content pillars.

6. Marketing Analytics Dashboard

Every dollar you spend on advertising should be traceable to a result. The Marketing Pro+ analytics dashboard provides a unified view of all paid activity across your connected Meta ad accounts, whether that activity originated from a full campaign, a manual boost, or an auto-boost rule.

Spend Tracking

The spend overview shows your total expenditure for the current billing period, broken down by day, week, or month. You can filter by platform (Facebook vs. Instagram), by campaign type (campaign, manual boost, auto-boost), and by audience template. A running total and daily average help you pace your budget throughout the month.

ROI Metrics

Marketing Pro+ calculates key return-on-investment metrics automatically:

  • Cost per result (CPR) — The average cost for each desired action (click, engagement, impression) based on your campaign objective.
  • Cost per click (CPC) — Tracked for all traffic-objective campaigns and any boost with link clicks.
  • Cost per thousand impressions (CPM) — The standard display metric, useful for comparing awareness campaign efficiency.
  • Return on ad spend (ROAS) — If you connect conversion tracking through the Meta Pixel, Marketing Pro+ calculates ROAS by dividing attributed revenue by ad spend.
  • Engagement rate on paid reach — The percentage of people who saw your ad and interacted with it, a valuable signal of creative quality.

Campaign Performance Table

Below the summary metrics, a sortable table lists every campaign, boost, and auto-boost with its status, spend, reach, impressions, clicks, engagement, CPR, and ROAS. You can click into any row to see a detailed breakdown including audience demographics that engaged, placement performance (Feed vs. Stories vs. Reels), and a timeline chart of daily results. This level of granularity lets you identify exactly which audience-creative combinations are working and reallocate budget accordingly.

Per-Platform Breakdown

A dedicated tab splits all metrics by Facebook and Instagram, so you can compare platform performance side by side. This is especially useful if you run the same creative on both platforms and want to determine where your budget is more effective. The breakdown includes platform-specific metrics such as Instagram Saves and Facebook Shares, which are not directly comparable but are each valuable indicators of content resonance on their respective platforms.

7. Getting Started with Marketing Pro+

Setting up Marketing Pro+ takes about ten minutes. Here is the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Enable the Add-On

Navigate to your Configure News account settings and open the Add-Ons section. Find Marketing Pro+ and click "Enable." You will be prompted to confirm the $19/month charge per ad account. The add-on is billed through your existing Configure News payment method (Lemon Squeezy or Paddle, depending on your account).

Step 2: Connect Your Meta Ad Account

After enabling the add-on, you will be guided through a Meta Business authorization flow. This grants Configure News permission to manage ads on your behalf through the Marketing API. You must have admin or advertiser access to the Facebook Business account and ad account you want to connect. If you already have Facebook or Instagram connected for organic publishing, Configure News will detect your existing authorization and request the additional ads_management permissions without requiring a full re-authentication.

Step 3: Create Your First Audience Template

Before launching any campaigns, build at least one audience template. Open the Audience Templates section, click "New Template," and define your targeting parameters. We recommend starting with a template that matches your core customer demographic: the geography, age range, and interests that describe the people most likely to engage with your content. Save the template with a descriptive name like "Core Audience - US Tech Professionals."

Step 4: Boost an Existing Post

The fastest way to see Marketing Pro+ in action is to boost a post that is already performing well organically. Go to your published posts, find one with above-average engagement, click the Boost button, select your new audience template, set a modest budget ($5-10), and confirm. Within minutes, your post will begin receiving paid reach and you will see results flowing into the analytics dashboard.

Step 5: Set Up Your First Auto-Boost Rule

Once you are comfortable with manual boosting, create your first auto-boost rule. Start conservative: choose a trigger metric like "50+ likes in 4 hours," set a low daily budget cap ($10-15), and select your core audience template. Let the rule run for a week, then review which posts it boosted and how they performed. Refine the threshold and budget based on real data.

Step 6: Launch a Full Campaign

When you are ready for more control, use the campaign builder to create a structured ad campaign. Choose your objective, define the budget and schedule, select or upload creative, and launch. Monitor the campaign daily in the analytics dashboard for the first few days, then shift to weekly reviews once performance stabilizes.

Marketing Pro+ is designed to grow with you. Start with manual boosts to build intuition for what works, graduate to auto-boost rules to automate your best practices, and use full campaigns when you need structured, objective-driven advertising. The analytics dashboard ties everything together so every decision is backed by data.
